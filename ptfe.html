PTFE Gaskets

A PTFE (Polytetrafluoroethylene) gasket is a type of gasket made from PTFE material, PTFE is a synthetic fluoropolymer known for its excellent chemical resistance, low friction, and non-stick properties. It is often used in gasket applications to enhance the performance and sealing capabilities of the gasket.

PTFE gaskets are compatible with various flange types, including raised face, flat face, and more. The specifications should indicate which types of flanges the gasket is suitable for.

  • PTFE gaskets usually have a smooth surface finish that contributes to their non-stick properties. This finish also helps prevent sticking to flanges during installation and removal.

  • PTFE gaskets come in various shapes and sizes to fit different flange configurations and applications. The specifications will define the outer and inner dimensions, bolt hole sizes, and thickness of the gasket.

  • PTFE is highly resistant to a wide range of chemicals, acids, bases, and solvents.

  • PTFE gaskets can handle a wide range of temperatures.

  • PTFE gaskets offer excellent sealing properties, especially in applications involving corrosive or aggressive media

  • PTFE is an excellent electrical insulator. Some PTFE gaskets have an anti-creep design, which helps to maintain their position during thermal cycling and varying pressures.
