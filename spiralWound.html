Spiral-Wound-Gaskets

Engineered with precision, our Spiral Wound Gaskets provide outstanding resilience and flexibility. Their unique design allows for excellent compression and recovery properties, ensuring a tight and reliable seal in any circumstance.

Spiral wound gaskets are designed and produced in manner to even recover under fluctuating loads during process pressure and temperature variations. A pre-formed metal winding strip combined with layered compressible material is used as sealing. The compressible material is allowed to fill the imperfections in the surfaces of the flange in which the gasket is used for sealing. Mechanical resistance is provided by the Outer metallic strip that holds the filler material.

Materials used in Spiral Wound Gaskets

Spiral wound metal gaskets can be customized with different materials to suit specific applications. The choice of materials depends on factors such as temperature, pressure, and chemical compatibility. Here are some commonly used materials:

Materials Application
Stainless Steel Petrochemical, oil and gas refineries
Carbon Steel General Industrial Applications
Iconel High-Temperature Applications
PTFE Chemical processing, food industry Chemical processing, food industry

Types of Spiral Wound Gaskets

Spiral wound gaskets are available in various styles, each designed for specific flange connections and service conditions. The most common types include Style CG, Style CGI, Style R, and Style RIR.

  • Style CG gaskets consist of a sealing element and an outer metal ring. They are suitable for raised and flat-faced flanges in mild to moderate service conditions.

  • Style CGI gaskets have an additional inner metal ring that acts as a compression stop and provides enhanced sealing performance.

  • Can withstand extreme temperatures, making them ideal for heat-exposed applications.

  • Style R gaskets feature metallic plies at the start and termination of the winding process, offering improved stability and sealing performance.

  • Style RIR gaskets combine a sealing element with an inner metal ring to prevent flange erosion and debris build-up.
